Core Equations You Must Own

These are the equations I expect you to be able to write cold, with no notes.

Demand

\(\theta^* = \frac{1}{\alpha}\Sigma^{-1}(\mu - p)\)

Equilibrium Price

\(p = \mu - \frac{1}{T}\Sigma \bar{\theta}\)

Live Walkthrough

Learn Mode gives full narration. Exam Mode hides the hints so you can rehearse.

1. Setup the economy

Each agent has CARA utility: \(U(c) = -\exp(-\alpha c)\). The risky payoff vector \(X\) is jointly normal with mean \(\mu\) and covariance \(\Sigma\).

State utility, distribution, and notation.

\(X \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma)\)

2. Solve expected utility

CARA + normal implies a certainty equivalent. Completing the square yields \(CE = \E[c] - \tfrac{\alpha}{2} \Var(c)\). This is your bridge to mean-variance.

Write the certainty equivalent.

\(CE = \E[c] - \frac{\alpha}{2}\Var(c)\)

3. Derive individual demand

Wealth is \(c = w_0 - p'\theta + \theta' X\). Substitute into CE, take the gradient, and solve: \(\theta^* = \frac{1}{\alpha}\Sigma^{-1}(\mu - p)\).

FOC and demand.

4. Market clearing

Sum demands across agents. Define total risk tolerance \(T = \sum_h 1/\alpha_h\). Set aggregate demand equal to supply \(\bar{\theta}\).

Aggregate and solve for prices.

\(p = \mu - \Sigma \bar{\theta}/T\)

5. Risk premia

Expected excess payoffs are proportional to covariance with aggregate risk. The price of risk is \(1/T\). More tolerance, higher prices, smaller premia.

Interpret \(1/T\).

6. Narrative for exam day

Say it out loud: preferences imply demand; demand aggregates; market clearing pins prices; prices imply premia. That is the whole story.

Give the one-sentence story.

Problem Walkthroughs

Each problem mirrors a qualifier prompt. Expand to see the full logic and checkpoints.

Write wealth as \(c = w_0 - p'\theta + \theta'X\). Use the CARA-normal CE to get \(CE = w_0 - p'\theta + \theta'\mu - \tfrac{\alpha}{2}\theta'\Sigma\theta\). Differentiate and solve for \(\theta^*\).

Aggregate \(\theta_h^*\) across agents. Define total risk tolerance \(T = \sum_h 1/\alpha_h\). Set \(\sum_h \theta_h^* = \bar{\theta}\) and solve for \(p = \mu - \Sigma \bar{\theta}/T\).

Compute \(\E[X] - p = \Sigma \bar{\theta}/T\). Interpret it as the price of risk times covariance with aggregate exposure. Only systematic risk is priced.

Show how prices rise with larger \(T\) and fall with higher \(\Sigma\). Ask how a more risk-averse agent shifts equilibrium prices.
